5 of My Favorite Foods from Malang

Bakso Malang

Meatballs are one of the most well-known and popular culinary specialties of Malang. Malang meatballs have a wider variety of contents than meatballs in other cities.

In addition to smooth meatballs which are more often called pentol, there are also meatballs with veins or coarse balls, fried long or flower dumplings, yellow noodles, vermicelli, and tofu. In addition, rice cake is usually served as a friend to eat meatballs.

Meatballs can be easily found in the city of Malang. Ranging from street carts to restaurants. The variety of meatballs provided is even more diverse. For example, firecracker meatballs, seafood meatballs, sum-sum meatballs, and rolled meatballs.

Mendol

The next typical Malang food is processed tempeh which is called mendol. Mendol is made from crushed tempeh. mixed with spices, clenched into an oval shape, then fried until crispy.

Usually this mendol is used as a side dish to complement pecel rice, rice corn, or rawon. It tastes savory with the aroma of kencur and leaves orange.

Cilok Daging

Cilok is better known as a typical Bandung snack. But there is also a typical Malang culinary called cilok meat.

Cilok which is sold on the roadside generally has a slightly rough texture like mini veined meatballs. Some are served with red sauce, peanut sauce, to meatball sauce and vegetables. There are also those that are burned with sweet soy sauce, you know.

Mie Setan

Currently, devil noodles or devil noodles can be found in various cities in Indonesia. But initially this food was pioneered in Malang. So it's not wrong if the devil's noodle is called a typical food of Malang.

A bowl of mie devil consists of thin egg noodles prepared like cwimie, but mixed with ground cayenne pepper. The level of spiciness can be adjusted according to the customer's taste. Usually this food seller also provides devil noodles mixed with cayenne pepper and sweet soy sauce as a menu variation.

Hundreds of shops offering devil's noodles can be found in Malang. Some of the popular ones among foodies are Kober Mie Setan, Mie Setan Noodle n' Dimsum, and Mie Gacoan.

Tahu Lontong

Surabaya has a special culinary called Tahu Lontong. However, there is also a typical Malang food called Tahu Lontong. Basically, Tahu Lontong is not much different from Tahu Tek. Even so, the typical Malang tofu lontong has a slightly different appearance and taste from Surabaya's tofu lontong.

However, the seasoning of tofu lontong in Malang is mixed with mashed peanuts and sweet soy sauce. After that, it is served with fried tofu, bean sprouts, and starch crackers. Meanwhile, tofu lontong in Surabaya is more dominant with petis and served with short bean sprouts.
